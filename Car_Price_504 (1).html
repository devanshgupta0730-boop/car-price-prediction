Business Context¶

The customer of this project is a car dealership/vehicle distribution company that should have a more precise idea of the car market to be able to make strategic choices. There are various types of vehicles that the company sells and it has to make decisions regarding the kind of vehicles to be stocked, the kind of price range to concentrate on and the kind of brands to concentrate on. The competition in the market is intense, preferences of customers evolve, and pricing can influence the profitability (Moraga-Gonzalez et al., 2023). The management thus needs a thorough analysis of the data that will show the relationship between the vehicle features, the brand reputation, fuel efficiency, engine performance, and the size of the vehicle to price and market popularity.

The company is exposed to a decision issue, which deals with market positioning and selection of the product. There are vehicles that make high profit but they sell low and there are those which sell high but make lower profit per unit. The management should thus be aware of the correlation between price, popularity, performance, and car features (Fatima et al., 2024). The data contains details regarding vehicle characteristics, brand popularity and price that can be examined to determine customer preference tendencies and market structure. Knowledge of these trends assists the company in determining the populations to target luxury cars, middle and low-end cars.

This analysis aims at converting vehicle data into business intelligence that enables strategic planning. The analysis will aim to find out the number of vehicle segments dominating the market, features that affect the price, positioning of brands in various segments of the market, and the market opportunities. This study will be used to enhance the company in terms of inventory planning, price determination, product positioning and market targeting. The last objective is to enable data-driven decision making and lessen business risk through application of market evidence rather than assumptions.

Dataset Characteristics¶

The data to be analyzed in this paper includes the data on various vehicles and their technical and other market-oriented aspects. All those rows signify the model of a vehicle and all columns define the feature of a vehicle which is brand, engine, fuel efficiency, vehicle size, vehicle style, popularity, and the manufacturer suggested retail price (MSRP). The data will contain both continuous and nominal variables, which will provide an opportunity to examine the price trends, market dynamics, features of performance, and positioning in the market. Some of the numerical variables are engine horsepower, engine cylinders, fuel efficiency, popularity, year, and price. Categorical variables encompass vehicle brand, fuel type, transmission type, vehicle size, vehicle style and drive wheels.

The initial dataset had 11,914 records and 16 variables. The sizes of the data were decreased after cleaning and elimination of redundant data. More features were developed in the process of feature engineering to enhance analysis. These new features are; car age, average fuel efficiency, horsepower per cylinder, door category, and popularity category. These artificial characteristics are useful in characterizing vehicle performance, performance, depreciation as well as market demand. The data is thus a powerful foundation to segment the market, price, brand positioning and identification of the market opportunity.

The analysis analysis in the future aims to involve the use of exploratory data analysis to provide answers to the business questions regarding the market structure, pricing behaviour, customer preferences and brand positioning. The second phase of the project is the vehicle size distribution, price distribution, vehicle style pricing, fuel efficiency and performance relationship, market opportunity across vehicle styles and brand positioning in various market segments and price versus popularity relationship. Data Quality Issues¶

A number of data quality problems were defined in the process of first exploration of the dataset. Such problems were missing values in various variables, duplicates, and large numbers of unique categories in variables. Such issues might have implications on the dependability of the statistical analysis, market segmentation and price analysis and hence data cleaning was necessary to proceed with the analysis.

There were missing values in some of the variables but the most notable problem was the Market Category variable which contained 3,742 missing values, which is very close to 31.4 percent of the dataset. This was a huge percentage and might influence category based analysis and market segmentation. The other variables that had missing values were Engine HP (69 missing values), Engine Cylinders (30 missing values), Number of Doors (6 missing values), and Engine Fuel Type (3 missing values). The level of missing values of these variables was not very high, but missing numerical values may influence average calculations, correlations, and price analysis and missing categorical values may influence grouping and category comparison.

The next critical data quality problem was that there were 715 duplicate rows in the data. Reproduction of records may corrupt statistical summaries like mean price, popularity distribution, and frequency counts. Without deleting duplicates, that would give a higher representation of some of the vehicles in the analysis and may give us false business information. Thus, duplicate records had to be eliminated prior to analysis.

The Model column was also determined as a possible problem since it had a very high number of unique values (915 unique models). This variable would not yield itself to grouped analysis and would be an unnecessary complication to the market analysis of the dataset. Hence, it was eliminated in the process of cleaning of data.

Data Cleaning and Pre-Processing Steps¶

A number of activities on data cleaning and preprocessing were conducted to make the dataset ready to analysis. It needed such steps to enhance the quality of the data, maintain its consistency, and to generate more variables which would be used in business analysis and in the market segmentation.

The initial process was to determine and eliminate duplicated records. The dataset had 715 duplicate records that may distort statistical analysis and interpretations and give wrong findings as some records of vehicles may be included in the dataset more than once. These were the duplicate records which were eliminated in order to have one record in every vehicle.

The second step was to deal with missing values. Both numeric and nominal variables had missing values. The empty values of the Engine HP and Engine Cylinders were filled using the median since the median is less sensitive to extreme values and it gives a more resilient estimate of numerical data. The mode was used to fill in the number of Doors and Engine Fuel Type since the variables are categorical or discrete by nature. The missing values of the Market Category variable were very numerous and this is why instead of deleting the rows, they were filled by the category of the unknown and this maintained the size of the dataset but still showed the information of the missing category.

Data types were realigned after processing values of missing data. Number of Doors and Engine Cylinders were transformed in integers due to the nature of the variables namely Number of Doors and Engine Cylinders; they are discrete variables and not continuous. The Model column was dropped since it had a very high number of distinct values and was not adding much value to the analysis of the market levels. The elimination of this column made the data simpler and minimized the complexity.

The final shape of the data is 11199 rows and 15 columns

Feature Engineering¶

The feature engineering was done to generate new variables that might have a better ability to capture vehicle characteristics and market behaviour as they are not easily available in the original dataset. These initial variables gave raw technical data like engine horsepower, fuel efficiency and year but the variables failed to tell directly about other concepts like the age of various vehicles, the overall fuel efficiency of the vehicles, engine efficiency of the vehicle and market popularity segments. Thus, there were new variables developed and to give the dataset more meaning in terms of business analysis and market segmentation.

The variable of the car age was formulated out of the year variable to indicate the depreciation of the vehicles and their lifecycle. This variable is beneficial to comprehend the relation of older vehicle to price and positioning in the market. The average MPG variable was developed by combining the city and highway fuel efficiency to develop one variable of fuel efficiency later on added in the bubble chart that represented the relationship between fuel efficiency, engine power and price. The Horsepower per Cylinder variable was designed to reflect the efficiency of the engine and not merely on the size of the engine and it aids in explaining the efficiency of the performance of various cars.

The Doors Category It ranked the vehicles as two doors and four-doors and that has made it easy to analyze the type of vehicles and customer preferences. The Popularity Category variable reflected the vehicles into the low, middle, and high popular segments and it was used to segment the market and to learn the distribution of the demand among various vehicles and brands.

Critical Analysis, Interpretations, Limitations, Business Recommendations and Conclusion¶

The car data analysis demonstrates that there are a number of noteworthy tendencies pertaining to market structure, price behaviour, segmentation and positioning. The market distribution analysis reveals that there is a greater number of compact and midsize cars than the large and luxury cars combined, which means that the automobile market is characterized by practical, affordable cars, rather than large or luxury car. The analysis of price distribution is heavily right skewed with majority of cars being lower than the mid-range prices with only a few vehicles being spread in price ranges very high. This implies that the car business is more of a quantity market than a luxury business. The connection between engine power, fuel consumption, and the price demonstrate a distinct trade-off between the performance and efficiency implying that the manufacturers have to find a balance between the two aspects based on the market segment. The brand positioning analysis also reveals that it is possible to classify brands into mass-market, premium, and luxury markets, where mass-market brands are the most popular and luxury ones are very price-sensitive and not demand-sensitive.

However, the dataset and analysis have a number of limitations. Actual sales volume, profit margins, customer demographics and cost of production are not provided in the data, thus restricting the capability of drawing direct conclusions on profitability. Demand is estimated using popularity which is a proxy of brand recognition and not necessarily sales performance. There is also a lack of vehicles of various years in the dataset, and the inflation or the change in the prices with time have not been factored, and this could influence the price comparison. Also, no geographic market information, level of income of customers or competitors strategies is provided as part of the data and this is a major factor in the actual automobile market analysis. That is why the analysis is helpful to obtain the insights into the market, but it must be regarded as indicative instead of definitive.

Relying on the insights obtained with the help of the examination, it is possible to formulate a number of business recommendations. With compact and midsize vehicles, companies should target them since the two segments take the greatest share of the market. The mid-range cars also seem to offer the optimal price to popularity ratio, implying that the companies should consider placing the products on the mid-price range instead of prioritizing the luxury cars. The sedans and SUVs seem to portray good market potential given that they are of good popularity and moderate pricing. Businesses must also aim at providing median engine-powered vehicles of good fuel efficiency to attract more customers. Production of luxury vehicles can still be carried out in order to position the brand and make sales in high margin, but this should not be the key focus of the market. According to the brand positioning analysis, it becomes important that a company make a clear choice on whether it would like to compete in the mass market segment or premium segment or luxury segment and not to compete in all segments at the same time.

In this regard, the exploratory data analysis provided information about the automobile market structure, pricing trends, vehicle segmentation, and brand positioning strategies. It was revealed that the compact and midsize car segment of the low to mid-price range dominated the automobile industry; luxury cars can be found in niche segments that have high prices and low popularity. Vehicle size and engine power were determined to have a significant impact on price whereas fuel efficiency was linked to cheaper cars. The brand positioning analysis showed that there was a distinct division in mass-market, premium and luxury brands. The analysis can hence give practical strategy to automobile companies and dealerships on how to position the product, pricing strategy, and market segmentation but further analysis would be needed using other data that would give more accurate business decision making, including sales volume and profit margins.
